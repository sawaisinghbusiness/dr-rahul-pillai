Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do results last?
Results vary by treatment. Laser hair removal results are permanent (90–95% reduction). Scar and pigmentation improvements are long-lasting. Botox lasts 4–6 months; fillers 12–18 months. Hair transplant results are permanent. Maintenance sessions help sustain all results long-term.
Are results permanent?
Some treatments offer permanent results — laser hair removal, hair transplant and scar treatments provide lasting improvement. Aesthetic injectables are temporary by design and require periodic maintenance. Dr. Pillai will clearly explain the longevity of results for your specific treatment during consultation.
Is laser treatment safe for Indian skin?
Absolutely. Our clinic uses multi-wavelength laser platforms specifically calibrated for all Fitzpatrick skin types, including darker Indian skin tones (Types IV–VI). A thorough consultation and test patch is always performed before any laser treatment to ensure complete safety.
How many sessions will I need?
Sessions needed depend on the treatment and your individual concern. Laser hair removal: 6–8 sessions. Acne scars: 4–6 sessions. Pigmentation: 4–6 sessions. PRP/GFC: 4–6 sessions. Dr. Pillai provides a personalised treatment plan with a clear session estimate during your first consultation.
Is there any downtime after treatment?
Most treatments have minimal to no downtime — laser hair removal, PRP, HydraFacial and mild peels allow immediate return to daily activities. Deeper treatments like fractional CO2 laser or medium peels may cause 3–7 days of redness or peeling. Your specific downtime will be discussed before treatment begins.
When will I see visible results?
Some treatments show immediate results — Botox is visible within 3–7 days; fillers and HydraFacial are instant. Laser and scar treatments show progressive improvement over 4–12 weeks as the skin heals and regenerates. Hair treatments typically show visible improvement from 3–6 months. Dr. Pillai will set realistic expectations for your timeline.
